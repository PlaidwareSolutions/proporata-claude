Build an actual  web app for a residential community and its HOA board

The goal is to show how a 144-unit HOA with 25 buildings can digitize documents, track work orders, visualize property health, and prepare for future community management application integration like vantaca.com.

This should look polished, professional, and client-ready.

# Product Name: HOA Operations Hub

# Target Customer

An HOA community with:
- 144 residential units
- 25 buildings
- Board members who need better continuity
- Property manager involvement
- Legacy paper records
- Roof, insurance, and correspondence documentation needs


# Initial Personas 
1. Board Members (Decision Makers)
Who they are:
* HOA President, Treasurer, Secretary, Directors
What they care about:
* Big picture visibility
* Costs, budgets, risk
* Compliance and documentation
* Vendor performance
What they need in your app:
* Dashboard (health of buildings, issues)
* Reports (maintenance trends, costs)
* Access to all documents
* Approval workflows (big decisions)
Reality check:
They are not technical and don’t want complexity.
If your UI confuses them, your product dies.

2. Property Manager (Operational Backbone)
Who they are:
* External management company (like Vantaca users)
* Handles day-to-day operations
What they care about:
* Efficiency
* Communication with board
* Vendor coordination
* Documentation
What they need:
* Work order management
* Document upload & organization
* Communication logs
* Reporting to HOA board
Reality:
They may resist your system if it creates extra work.
Your system must reduce their workload or integrate with theirs.


# Core Business Problems

This should communicate that the system solves:

1. Scattered paper files and lost documentation
2. Loss of visibility when property managers change
3. Manual folder creation and manual document tracking
4. Reactive maintenance instead of proactive planning
5. No easy way to see building/unit health
6. No centralized history for roofs, insurance, work orders, or correspondence

# Tech Stack

Use:
- Next.js
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS
- shadcn/ui components if available
- Lucide icons
- No authentication required at the moment
